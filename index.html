How it gets in the jar

Hives follow the seasons. We pull frames by hand and don't blend batches together.

  1. Forage

    Bees forage

    Hives follow the bloom. Orange in spring, alfalfa in summer, hillsides when the rain holds.

  2. Harvest

    We harvest

    Frames only when they're capped. We leave enough on the hive for winter.

  3. Jar & label

    Jar & label

    Numbered, noted, and tasted at our table in El Cajon. Grandpa still signs off.

The full process

Two generations, one table

The Honey Lab is run by my grandfather and me out of El Cajon. He kept bees before I was born. I jar everything by hand and write down what each batch tastes like.

Most days the lab is our kitchen table: jars in a row, a pen, and a notebook full of hive numbers.
